2025: In the EuroBasket 2025 final, Germany defeated Turkey 88–83 to claim its second title. The tournament was co-hosted by Cyprus, Finland, Latvia, and Poland. Germany's point guard, Dennis Schröder, was named the FIBA EuroBasket MVP.
2022: Spain won its fourth EuroBasket title by defeating France in the final. The final game took place in Berlin, and Willy Hernangómez of Spain was named the tournament's MVP.
2017: Slovenia secured its first-ever EuroBasket title by beating Serbia 93–85 in the final. The tournament was co-hosted by Finland, Israel, Romania, and Turkey. Goran Dragić of Slovenia was the MVP.
2015: Hosted by Croatia, France, Germany, and Latvia, Spain won their third title by defeating Lithuania 80–63 in the final. Pau Gasol of Spain was named the tournament's MVP.
2013: France won its first European title by defeating Lithuania in the final held in Slovenia. France's Tony Parker was named the MVP.
2011: Held in Lithuania, Spain emerged victorious over France to win their second consecutive EuroBasket title. Pau Gasol was once again named the tournament's MVP.
2009: Spain claimed its first EuroBasket title by defeating Serbia 85–63 in the final held in Poland. Pau Gasol was also the MVP for this tournament.
2007: In a one-point victory, Russia took their first EuroBasket title since the dissolution of the Soviet Union by defeating hosts Spain 60–59 in the final. Russia's Andrei Kirilenko was the MVP.
2005: Hosted by Serbia and Montenegro, Greece won its second European title by defeating Germany 78–62 in the final. Dirk Nowitzki of Germany was voted the tournament's MVP.
